1.5 Time stepping and error control

We have seen that the error of the numerical solution will decrease as we decrease the time step. In particular, we have shown that the global error of the Forward Euler method will decrease linearly with the time step size: ϵqN∼O(Δt).\epsilon_{\mathbf{q}_N}\sim \mathcal{O}(\Delta t).ϵqN​​∼O(Δt).

Can we control this error such that its value is always below a certain tolerance, τ\tauτ?

Let's look into more detail on how the error could be controled. We know that the truncation error of the state vector at time iii is

ϵqi=Δt22q¨i+O(Δt3)\epsilon_{\mathbf{q}_i} = \frac{\Delta t^2}{2}\ddot{\mathbf{q}}_i + \mathcal{O}(\Delta t^3)ϵqi​​=2Δt2​q¨​i​+O(Δt3)

and we want that ϵqi≤τ\epsilon_{\mathbf{q}_i}\leq\tauϵqi​​≤τ Assuming that the third-order term will vanish faster, we seek for a variable time step size such that
